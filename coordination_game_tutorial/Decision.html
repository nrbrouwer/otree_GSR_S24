Practice Instructions

In this practice game, the computer will assign you a color at the beginning, either Blue or Red. You will then be given the option to select either Blue or Red for 10 rounds of this game. The goal of this game is to select the color that matches the color you were assigned at the beginning of the game. Your assigned color does not change between rounds. So, for example, if you were assigned the color Red, your assigned color would be Red in all 10 rounds.

The challenge of this game is that you will not be told what color you were assigned at the beginning. Instead, at the beginning of each round, you will be given a signal color that indicates what your assigned color might be. There is a 66% chance that the signal color will match the color you were assigned. Likewise, the is a 33% chance that the signal color will be the opposite color.

When you select the color you were assigned at the beginning, you will receive {{ correct_points }} points. When you select the color that does not match your assigned color, you will receive {{ incorrect_points }} points. You will not be told how many points you have earned in this practice game until you finish all 10 rounds.
